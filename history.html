HISTORY

Nintendo wasn’t always like it is now in the 90s. Take a look back at its origins and early milestones...

1889: Nintendo's Humble Beginnings

Nintendo was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Japan. It started as a company producing handmade Hanafuda playing cards, which were popular for gambling and entertainment.

For decades, Nintendo remained focused on card games, eventually becoming a household name in Japan.

1950s: Expansion and Western Influence

In the 1950s, Nintendo expanded its card business by partnering with companies like Disney, printing characters like Mickey Mouse on their cards to appeal to younger audiences.

This marked the beginning of Nintendo’s creative approach to family entertainment.

1960s: Moving into Toys

During the 1960s, Nintendo moved beyond playing cards into toys and novelty products. This was a period of experimentation with items like:

  • Ultra Hand (1966) – An extendable toy hand that was a commercial success.
  • Love Tester (1969) – A quirky device that "measured" love between two people.

This creative spirit laid the foundation for Nintendo’s focus on fun and innovative entertainment.

1970s: Stepping into Electronics

By the 1970s, Nintendo embraced electronics and arcade games. Key milestones include:

  • 1973: Nintendo released its first laser clay shooting systems, a precursor to their interactive entertainment systems.
  • 1977: The Color TV-Game series launched in Japan, marking Nintendo's entry into the home console market.
  • 1979: Gunpei Yokoi introduced handheld games, leading to the creation of the Game & Watch series.

The Game & Watch line was a hit, combining portability with gaming and laying the groundwork for future handheld systems.

1981: Donkey Kong and the Birth of Mario

Nintendo hit the global stage with the release of Donkey Kong in arcades. The game introduced two icons:

  • Donkey Kong – The game’s villain.
  • Jumpman – Later renamed Mario, he became Nintendo's mascot and the face of the company.

1983: The Famicom and NES Revolution

In 1983, Nintendo launched the Family Computer (Famicom) in Japan. It was a home console that could play cartridge-based games.

By 1985, Nintendo released the Famicom internationally as the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). The NES revitalized the video game industry in North America after the crash of 1983.

Iconic games like Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda became household staples, turning Nintendo into a gaming juggernaut.

1989: Game Boy – Portable Gaming

The Game Boy, designed by Gunpei Yokoi, was released in 1989. It brought gaming on the go with titles like:

  • Tetris – A puzzle game that became synonymous with the Game Boy.
  • Super Mario Land – Mario’s first handheld adventure.

The Game Boy sold millions of units, cementing Nintendo’s dominance in handheld gaming.

Fun Facts

  • Did you know Nintendo started in 1889 as a card company?
  • The NES (1985) saved the video game market from collapse in North America.
  • Mario was originally called Jumpman in Donkey Kong.
  • Nintendo’s Game Boy sold over 118 million units worldwide.
